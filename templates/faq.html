Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our YouTube downloader

Is this tool free to use?

Yes, this YouTube downloader is completely free. There are no hidden fees, premium plans, or download limits. It will always remain free for personal and educational use.

Is it safe to use?

Absolutely. We don't store any of your data, don't require registration, and all downloads are processed securely. Files are temporarily stored during processing and immediately deleted after delivery to you.

Why can't some videos be downloaded?

Several reasons might prevent downloads:

  • Age restrictions: Videos with age gates may require authentication
  • Geographic restrictions: Some content is region-locked
  • Copyright protection: Certain videos have enhanced DRM protection
  • Private videos: Videos set to private cannot be accessed
  • Live streams: Active live streams may not download correctly

Which formats are supported?

We support two primary formats:

  • MP4 (Video): Download full video with audio in resolutions from 360p to 4K (2160p)
  • MP3 (Audio): Extract audio-only files in bitrates from 96 kbps to 320 kbps

Do I need to install anything?

No installation is required on your end. This is a web-based tool that works directly in your browser. However, the server requires FFmpeg for MP3 extraction and video merging.

Why is my download taking so long?

Download time depends on several factors:

  • Video length (longer videos take more time)
  • Selected quality (4K takes longer than 360p)
  • Server load and your internet connection speed
  • YouTube's server response time

Can I download playlists?

Currently, playlist downloads are not supported. You'll need to download videos one at a time by pasting individual video URLs.

Is this legal?

Downloading YouTube videos can be legal or illegal depending on how you use them. This tool is intended for:

  • Personal, non-commercial use
  • Educational purposes
  • Offline viewing of content you have rights to

Users must respect copyright laws and YouTube's Terms of Service. The developer is not responsible for any misuse of this tool.

What quality should I choose?

It depends on your needs:

  • 4K (2160p): Best quality, largest file size - for archival or large displays
  • 1080p: High quality, reasonable size - great for most uses
  • 720p: Good quality, smaller size - ideal for mobile devices
  • 360p/480p: Lower quality, smallest size - for previews or limited storage

Does this work on mobile devices?

Yes! Our tool is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. Simply open it in your mobile browser and use it the same way as on desktop.

Where are my downloads saved?

Downloads are saved to your browser's default download folder. You can change this location in your browser settings. On most browsers, you'll see a download notification when the file is ready.

Can I use this tool commercially?

No. This tool is strictly for personal and educational use only. Commercial use, redistribution, or using downloaded content for profit violates YouTube's Terms of Service and copyright laws.
